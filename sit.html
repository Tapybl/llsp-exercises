The sit to stand exercise helps to strengthen your core muscles, as well as the muscles in your thighs. Your core muscles surround your trunk and include your abdominals, obliques, diaphragm, pelvic floor, trunk extensors, and hip flexors. Your core provides stability to your trunk for balance and for movements like lifting weights and standing up from a chair.

To do the sit to stand exercise:

  • Sit in a sturdy chair that will not move with your feet shoulder-width apart.

  • Cross your arms across your chest with your hands resting on your shoulders.

  • Slowly and steadily push through your feet to a standing position.

  • Gently bend your knees in a slow, controlled movement to sit into the chair.

  • Take a break and repeat as many times as you’re comfortable.
